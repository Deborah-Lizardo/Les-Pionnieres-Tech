ABOUT THE ARTICLES

This page highlights groundbreaking articles and projects led by women in the field of assistive technology. The content includes various research papers and projects that are helping to shape a more accessible and inclusive digital environment.

These initiatives are focused on creating personalized assistive technologies, enhancing accessibility through wearable computing, and utilizing 3D printing to support individuals with disabilities.

1. "Designing for Accessibility: A Review of the Literature on Accessible Technology"

Authors: Jennifer Mankoff, et al.

Description: This paper provides a comprehensive review of the literature on the design of accessible technologies. It examines how technologies can be adapted to improve accessibility for people with various disabilities.

ACM Digital Library

2. "Personalized Assistive Technologies: The Role of Machine Learning in Accessibility"

Authors: Jennifer Mankoff, et al.

Description: This paper examines how machine learning can be applied to personalize assistive technologies for individuals, ensuring that solutions are tailored to meet the specific needs of each user with a disability.

IEEE Xplore

3. "Using Wearable Computing to Improve Accessibility for People with Disabilities"

Authors: Jennifer Mankoff, et al.

Description: This paper explores how wearable computing can be used to improve accessibility, offering examples of assistive devices that can help facilitate the interaction of people with motor or cognitive disabilities with their environments.

ACM Digital Library

4. "3D Printed Assistive Technologies for the Visually Impaired"

Authors: Jennifer Mankoff, et al.

Description: This paper details the use of 3D printing to create assistive technologies, such as devices for people with visual impairments. It explores how 3D printing can be used to create low-cost, customized devices that meet specific accessibility needs.
